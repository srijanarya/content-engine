# Standard disclaimers (append to published / sold market content)

## Short form (social posts, X/LinkedIn — one line)
> Market data & language analysis for education only. Not investment advice; no buy/sell/hold
> recommendation. Not a SEBI-registered research analyst or investment adviser.

## Long form (newsletter footer, paid report)
> **Disclaimer.** This is a data and language-analysis product for educational and informational
> purposes only. It summarises publicly available market data and public company disclosures. It is
> **not** a research report, **not** investment advice, and contains **no** buy/sell/hold
> recommendation, price target, or trading call on any security. The author is **not** a
> SEBI-registered Research Analyst or Investment Adviser. Nothing here should be relied upon as the
> basis for any investment decision; consult a SEBI-registered adviser and do your own research.
> Markets carry risk, including loss of capital.

## Why the wording matters (do not weaken)
SEBI does not treat a disclaimer as curative if the underlying content is a per-security
recommendation — the disclaimer protects ONLY content that is genuinely non-recommendatory.
So the disclaimer rides ON TOP of the linter, it does not replace it. The content must already be
clean (zero block-level lint violations) before the disclaimer is appended.
